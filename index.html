What to do if a rent increase is too high

The ACT has rules about how much rent can be increased.

If your landlord or property manager asks for a rent increase more than what is allowed, you can refuse the request.

Either you or the landlord can apply to the ACAT for a resolution.

If you are being asked for a rent increase above the allowable amount, consider contacting the ACT legal aid tenancy service for advice.

You can read more about the rules and how to apply for a review at the ACAT website.

You can ask questions and read about other people's experiences with rent increases on the Canberra Renters Facebook Group.

For general information about renting in Canberra, you can use the ACT Government's Renting Book (PDF)
